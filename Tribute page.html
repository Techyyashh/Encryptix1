Stephen Hawking

A tribute to the legendary physicist and cosmologist

Biography

Stephen William Hawking was an English theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author who was the director of research at the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Cambridge. His key works include theoretical predictions about black holes emitting radiation, now known as Hawking radiation, and his popular science books that explain complex scientific concepts to a general audience.

Timeline

  • 1942: Born in Oxford, England
  • 1963: Diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
  • 1974: Formulated the theory of Hawking radiation
  • 1988: Published "A Brief History of Time"
  • 2009: Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2018: Passed away in Cambridge, England
